1. Why is PPP preferred over HDLC in enterprise WANs?
Because it has authentication and multiprotocol encapsulation support.
2. What security advantage does CHAP have over PAP?
It never sends the password as a plain text.
3. Why must CHAP usernames match remote router hostnames?
Because it uses the hostname to authenticate the router.
4. What is the purpose of “interesting traffic” in IPSec?
It defines which packets will go through the VPN.
5. Difference between IKE Phase 1 and Phase 2?
Phase 1 establishes a secure channel between the peers, phase 2 encrypt the actual data traffic.
6. Why must crypto maps be applied to an interface?
Because a crypto map only processes traffic on the interface where it is applied.
7. What happens if IPSec ACLs do not match on both sides?
IPSec SA negotiation fails or traffic is dropped.
8. Difference between Site-to-Site and Remote-Access VPNs?
Site-to-Site connects entire networks, remote connects individually.
9. Can VPN replace NAT? Why or why not?
No. VPN provides secure tunneling, while NAT provides address translation; they solve different problems and serve different purposes.
